Quick Sort

Quick Sort is one of the most popular and efficient algorithms for sorting data. Sorting means arranging items in a particular order, like putting numbers from smallest to largest or words alphabetically. Quick Sort is called “quick” because it usually works faster than many other sorting methods. Here's how it works: imagine you have a pile of cards. You pick one card as a pivot (a reference point). Then, you separate the other cards into two groups: one with cards smaller than the pivot and one with cards larger. After that, you repeat the process for each group, choosing new pivots until everything is sorted. Quick Sort is widely used because it is fast and works well even with large amounts of data. It is a great example of how breaking a big problem into smaller steps makes it easier to solve.
